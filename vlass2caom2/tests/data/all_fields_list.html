<h2>VLA Sky Survey Quick Look Images</h2>

<p>
Quick Look image products are 1x1 degree images of the 2-4GHz Stokes
I continuum, and an associated image of the RMS.
</p>

<p>
Images are grouped by tiles, as described at <a
href="https://archive-new.nrao.edu/vlass/VLASS_dyn_summary.php">Tile
Definitions and Observing Status</a>, and individual images are are
named as follows:
</p>

<ul>
<li>
VLASS epoch (1.1=first half of first epoch; 1.2=second half of first epoch; 2.1=first half of second epoch, etc.)
</li>
<li>
Product type (ql=Quick Look imaging)
</li>
<li>
Tile name
</li>
<li>
Image phase center
</li>
<li>
Pixel size x10 (10=1.0arcsec)
</li>
<li>
Bandwidth in MHz
</li>
<li>
Version number
</li>
<li>
Stokes type
</li>
</ul>

<p>Quick Look images do not fully sample the PSF, and are cleaned to
a threshold of ~5 sigma (details can be found in the weblogs for
individual images). They are used for Quality
Assurance and for transient searches. They should not be used for
any other purpose.</p>

<p>UPDATE, Nov 2017: We have found a VLA system issue that is
resulting in "ghost" images of bright sources separated by
approximately 3 arcmin in RA. These can be either +RA or -RA,
depending on the scan direction of the affected OTF row. We are
investigating and will re-post corrected images as and when they are
identified.</p>
